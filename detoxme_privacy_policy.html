DetoxMe does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data with external servers, third parties, or advertisers. All data stays on your device.

3. Data we collect and store

DetoxMe stores the following data locally on your device only using Android Room (SQLite) database. This data never leaves your device.

Data type What it is Why it is stored Where it is stored
App package names The internal identifier of apps you choose to monitor (e.g., com.instagram.android) To know which apps to track and block when your time limit is reached Local Room database on your device
Daily usage time The number of minutes/seconds you have used each selected app per day To display your usage reports (daily, weekly, monthly) and trigger limits Local Room database on your device
Time limits set by user The daily screen time limits you configure per app To enforce app blocking when your chosen limit is reached Local Room database on your device

We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, location, contacts, photos, payment details, device identifiers, or any other personal information.

4. Permissions used

DetoxMe requires the following Android permissions to function. Each permission is used solely for core app functionality.

Permission Type Why it is needed
Accessibility Service Sensitive Used to monitor which app is currently in the foreground and to block the selected app (by redirecting away from it) when your configured daily time limit is exceeded. The Accessibility Service does not read, record, or transmit any screen content, text, passwords, or personal data.
Usage Stats (PACKAGE_USAGE_STATS) Sensitive Used to read the daily screen time of the apps you have selected to monitor. This data is read locally and stored only on your device.
Foreground Service Normal Keeps the monitoring service running in the background so usage is tracked accurately even when the app is not open.
Receive Boot Completed Normal Restarts the monitoring service automatically when your device is rebooted so your limits stay active.

Important note on Accessibility Service: The Accessibility Service in DetoxMe is used exclusively to detect the foreground app and block it when a time limit is reached. It does not capture, read, record, or transmit any text entered on screen, passwords, messages, or any other content from any app.

5. Data sharing and third parties

DetoxMe does not share any data with any third party. There are no analytics SDKs, advertising SDKs, crash reporting services, or cloud services integrated into the App.

Specifically, DetoxMe does not:

  • Send any data to external servers or the internet
  • Use Google Analytics, Firebase, or any analytics service
  • Use any advertising network or display ads
  • Integrate any social login or third-party authentication
  • Include any third-party SDKs that collect user data

6. Data retention and deletion

All data stored by DetoxMe (selected app package names and usage times) resides in a local Room database on your Android device. The data is retained until you:

  • Manually clear the app's data from Android Settings → Apps → DetoxMe → Clear Data
  • Uninstall the App (all app data is deleted by Android automatically)
  • Remove a specific app from monitoring within DetoxMe (deletes that app's record)

We have no access to your data and cannot delete it on your behalf, as it is stored entirely on your device.

7. Children's privacy

DetoxMe is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect any personal information from children. Since the App stores all data locally and collects no personal information, it poses no specific risk to children. Parents and guardians are encouraged to supervise app usage on devices used by children.

8. Data security

Since all data is stored locally on your device and never transmitted over a network, the security of your data is governed by your device's own security measures (screen lock, encryption, etc.). We recommend keeping your device updated and secured with a lock screen.

The App does not implement its own server-side security because no server is involved.
